## Content Guidelines: No BS, Facts Only

### DO ✅
- Use concrete numbers: "$197/mo", "5 hours/week", "98% accuracy"
- Show comparisons: "$797/mo vs $18,750/mo for 3 SDRs"
- Cite sources: "(verified by Nielsen Norman Group)"
- Set realistic expectations: "Handles 45-50% of routine queries"
- Emphasize control: "No lock-in—export anytime"

### DON'T ❌
- Use vague terms: "transform", "revolutionize", "disrupt"
- Over-promise: "Replace entire teams", "100% automation"
- Hide pricing: "Contact us for pricing"
- Use corporate speak: "synergies", "leverage", "paradigm shift"
- Make unverifiable claims: "10x productivity", "AI transformation"

### Example Headlines (Good vs Bad)

**Good:**
- "ship agents, not hours"
- "3 AI agents. 2 pricing tiers. 0 lock-in."
- "$197/mo to replace $6,250/mo in SDR costs"

**Bad:**
- "Transform your business with AI" (too vague)
- "Revolutionary AI agents" (over-hyped)
- "The future of work is here" (cringe)

## Red Flags from Market Research

### 1. 40% of Agentic AI Projects Will Fail by 2027
**Source:** Gartner 2025 Report
**Why:** Escalating costs, unclear business value
**How to avoid:**
- Set clear ROI expectations upfront
- Monthly pricing (not just one-time) for ongoing value
- Show concrete time/cost savings, not vague "transformation"

### 2. Only 30% Task Completion for Complex Work
**Source:** Carnegie Mellon 2025 Study
**Why:** AI agents struggle with multi-step, ambiguous tasks
**How to avoid:**
- Scope agents to specific workflows (outreach, meetings, support)
- Don't promise "replace entire teams"
- Emphasize human-in-the-loop where needed (support agent requires approval)

### 3. Only 19% of Companies See Significant ROI from AI
**Source:** Forrester 2024
**Why:** Lack of change management, poor integration
**How to avoid:**
- Offer managed tier (you handle integration)
- Provide clear deployment instructions (self-serve)
- Set 1-2 hour deployment expectation (realistic)
